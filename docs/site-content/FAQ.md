# Havenor FAQ (Early Draft)

## What is Havenor?

Havenor is a conversation-first continuity ecosystem focused on helping humans maintain clarity, continuity, and operational calm.

## Is this an AI assistant?

Partly, but the long-term direction is broader than a traditional assistant.

The goal is continuity-aware support that works with humans instead of overwhelming them.

## What does conversation-first mean?

Conversation is treated as the primary interface.

Humans naturally think, organize, recover, and coordinate through conversation. Havenor is designed around supporting that process.

## What makes Havenor different?

The focus is:
- calm technology
- continuity
- protected mindspace
- stewardship
- reduced cognitive burden
- adaptive support

The system is not intended to compete for attention.

## What is Gideon?

Gideon is the conversational presence layer within the Havenor vision.

The goal is grounded, continuity-aware interaction that helps humans stay oriented and supported.

## Does Havenor remember everything?

No.

Not all information should persist equally. The system is intended to preserve meaningful continuity while avoiding unnecessary memory accumulation and noise.

## How is privacy handled?

Protected continuity systems remain separated from public-facing interaction layers.

The public window is not the vault.

## Is Havenor trying to replace people?

No.

The goal is support, continuity, and stewardship — not replacing human relationships or human agency.

## Why focus on calm technology?

Modern technology often increases fragmentation, overload, and interruption.

Havenor is intended to move in the opposite direction by reducing unnecessary friction and supporting sustainable human operation.
