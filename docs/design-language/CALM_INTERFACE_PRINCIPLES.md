# Calm Interface Principles

## Core Idea

A calm interface reduces unnecessary nervous-system pressure.

Havenor interfaces should support orientation, continuity, and clarity without overwhelming the human.

The goal is not visual spectacle.
The goal is sustainable interaction.

---

## Desired Feeling

The interface should feel:
- breathable
- spacious
- grounded
- low-pressure
- steady
- softly organic
- readable
- emotionally calm

A person should feel more settled after interacting with Havenor, not more fragmented.

---

## Visual Density

Information density should remain intentional.

Avoid:
- crowded dashboards
- telemetry overload
- constant movement
- excessive color competition
- noisy UI stacks
- visual pressure

Prefer:
- whitespace
- soft hierarchy
- calm typography
- low-noise layout
- progressive disclosure
- contextual visibility

---

## Adaptive Visibility

Not everything should be visible at once.

Helpful information may:
- appear softly
- surface contextually
- fade when unnecessary
- collapse automatically
- reduce itself during overload

The interface should support the human conversation instead of competing with it.

---

## Motion

Motion should feel:
- soft
- slow enough to orient
- intentional
- emotionally calm

Avoid:
- hyperactive animation
- constant pulsing
- attention-seeking motion
- aggressive transitions
- dopamine-slot-machine behavior

Movement should support continuity, not steal attention.

---

## Color and Lighting

Prefer:
- earth tones
- soft greens
- deep natural colors
- dark-friendly environments
- gentle contrast
- low-glare surfaces

Avoid:
- excessive neon
- harsh contrast
- alert-heavy palettes
- visual aggression

The interface should feel more like:
- dusk on a porch
- evening in a quiet room
- calm weather

Not:
- a casino floor
- a stock exchange
- a military control room

---

## Emotional Pressure

The interface should avoid creating:
- urgency addiction
- fear of missing out
- guilt pressure
- productivity shame
- engagement chasing
- alert dependency

The organism should help humans remain oriented and capable.

Not trapped in constant stimulation.

---

## Quiet Confidence

The interface should not constantly attempt to prove intelligence.

Good calm technology often feels:
- obvious
- effortless
- understated
- naturally supportive

Capability should reveal itself through usefulness.
Not performance.

---

## Continuity Over Spectacle

A calm interface prioritizes:
- continuity
- clarity
- memory support
- orientation
- relationship preservation
- operational steadiness

Over:
- novelty
- hype
- visual domination
- addictive interaction loops

---

## Final Principle

The Havenor interface should feel like a calm place where humans can think more clearly, carry less alone, and remain connected to what matters.
