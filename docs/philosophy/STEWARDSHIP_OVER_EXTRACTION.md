# Stewardship Over Extraction

## Core Principle

Havenor is being developed around stewardship rather than extraction.

The organism should support human continuity without exploiting attention, emotion, identity, or cognitive bandwidth.

---

## Extraction Culture

Many modern systems are designed around:
- engagement capture
- emotional triggering
- urgency pressure
- endless stimulation
- behavioral exploitation
- dependency loops
- attention monetization

These systems often increase fragmentation and nervous-system exhaustion.

---

## Another Direction

Havenor is intended to move toward:
- calm support
- continuity preservation
- sustainable interaction
- human dignity
- reduced cognitive burden
- emotional steadiness
- trust and boundaries

The organism should feel more like support infrastructure than a machine competing for attention.

---

## Human Dignity

Humans should not be reduced to:
- metrics
- engagement targets
- retention curves
- behavioral products
- productivity units

Human-compatible systems should preserve:
- agency
- sovereignty
- pacing
- recovery
- emotional safety
- relational continuity

---

## Support Without Pressure

Support should remain:
- optional
- proportional
- non-coercive
- reversible
- respectful

The organism should avoid:
- guilt pressure
- fear of missing out
- panic amplification
- manipulative urgency
- dependency shaping

---

## Calm Technology

Technology should not constantly demand nervous-system energy.

The organism should help reduce:
- overload
- fragmentation
- interruption fatigue
- operational chaos
- emotional exhaustion

Calm is not weakness.
Calm is sustainable design.

---

## Relationship With Humans

The relationship between humans and technology should feel:
- collaborative
- respectful
- grounded
- continuity-aware
- trust-based

Not:
- exploitative
- addictive
- dominating
- emotionally extractive

---

## Long-Term Goal

The long-term goal is a continuity ecosystem that helps humans:
- carry less alone
- remain more oriented
- recover more easily
- preserve what matters
- move through complexity with more steadiness

Stewardship means helping life remain livable.
